Our mission

StoryCast was founded on a simple belief — that every story deserves to be heard, and every listener deserves equal access to hear it. Too often, audio and video content is published without transcripts or captions, excluding millions of people who are deaf, hard of hearing, or simply prefer to read.

"If your story can only be experienced one way, it isn't fully told."

We are committed to changing that — one story at a time. Every piece of audio content on StoryCast includes a full text transcript. Every video includes synchronized captions. Every interface element works with a keyboard and a screen reader. This is not optional, it is foundational.

Accessibility features

Full transcripts

Every audio story includes a complete text transcript — readable inline or downloadable as a .txt file.

Closed captions

All video stories include synchronized captions delivered via WebVTT <track> elements.

Keyboard navigation

The full site is usable without a mouse — tab order, skip links, arrow key controls, and focus management throughout.

Visible focus states

Every interactive element has a clear, high-contrast focus ring — minimum 3px, visible in all themes.

Color contrast

All text meets WCAG AA 4.5:1 ratio. Large text meets 3:1. Tested with axe DevTools and Colour Contrast Analyser.

Semantic HTML

Proper landmarks, ARIA roles, and heading hierarchy throughout every page for screen reader compatibility.
